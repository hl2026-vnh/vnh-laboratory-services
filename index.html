Welcome to VNH Laboratory Services

The Vanuatu National Hospital (VNH) Laboratory Department provides quality diagnostic services to support patient care, disease surveillance, and public health programs throughout Vanuatu. This website serves as a practical resource for clinicians and healthcare workers, providing information on available laboratory tests, specimen collection requirements, sample transport, referral testing, laboratory contacts, and service information.

Our laboratory team is committed to delivering accurate, reliable, and timely results that support the prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and monitoring of disease. Services are provided across a range of laboratory disciplines; Biochemistry, Haematology, Blood Bank and Transfusion, Microbiology, Serology, Tuberculosis, Histology, Cytology, Molecular, Phlebotomy, and Surveillance Testing.

For clinicians

How to request a sample

Routine and urgent requests are handled differently — make sure the request form reflects the true urgency.

  1. Complete the request form fully

    Include patient first name, surname, date of birth or age, hospital number (where applicable), gender, specimen type, collection date, date of symptom onset, and the requesting clinician's name.

  2. Flag urgent requests clearly

    Mark the form URGENT if the result is needed before the next routine reporting cycle and will directly affect patient management. Unmarked specimens are processed as routine.

  3. Use the correct container for the test

    Check the specimen criteria below — wrong or inappropriate containers are a common reason for specimen rejection.

  4. Send the specimen promptly

    Deliver to laboratory reception during normal working hours, or to the on-call laboratory scientist out of hours. A phone call ahead of an urgent or unusual specimen helps the lab prepare.

  5. For additional tests requested verbally

    Verbal add-on requests (e.g. by phone) must be made within 3 hours of specimen collection, and must include the additional test requested, the requesting clinician's name, and the date and time of the request.

Out-of-hours requests

Only request urgent analysis out of normal working hours if the result is needed before the next full working day and is likely to directly affect patient management. Specimens without a clear urgency note may be delayed or treated as routine — when in doubt, call the on-call laboratory scientist.

Before you send

Specimen criteria

Minimum acceptance criteria

  • Specimen and request form both carry a minimum of four patient identifiers (name, surname, DOB/age, hospital number, or gender)
  • Specimen type and collection date are recorded
  • Date of onset of symptoms is recorded
  • Requesting clinician's initials are present
  • Specimen is submitted in the correct, authorized container for that test
  • Sputum specimens are received in a biohazard transport bag

Common rejection reasons

  • Form or specimen missing the minimum identifiers above
  • Haemolysed sample for biochemistry, FBC, cross-match, or blood culture
  • Mislabelled or inappropriate specimen container
  • Clotted sample submitted for FBC
  • Sample drawn from an IV line for FBC or biochemistry
  • Neonatal bilirubin sample not wrapped in aluminium foil
  • Saliva submitted instead of sputum
  • Specimen received leaking, unsanitary, or with exterior contamination

Send-away testing

Tests referred for further testing

Tests marked SEND-AWAY in the directory above are not processed at VNH — they are referred out, and results take days to weeks rather than hours. Discuss timing with the relevant department before sending. If you need a test that isn't listed anywhere in the directory, contact the relevant department directly — they can advise whether it can be referred out.
